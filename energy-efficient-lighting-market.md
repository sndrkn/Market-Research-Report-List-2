<p><p>The energy-efficient lighting market consists of different types, including High-Intensity Discharge Lamps (HID), Linear Fluorescent Lamps (LFL), and Light Emitting Diodes (LED). HID lamps are efficient light sources that produce light by passing an electric current through a gas and emit a bright, white light. LFLs are long, tubular lamps that produce light by passing an electric current through mercury vapor, creating ultraviolet light that excites phosphor coating inside the lamp. LEDs are small, solid-state devices that produce light by passing an electric current through a semiconductor material, emitting light in a specific color.</p></p>
