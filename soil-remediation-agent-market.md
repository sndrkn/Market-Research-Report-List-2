<p><p>Soil remediation agents are substances used to treat contaminated soil and restore its quality. Organic soil remediation agents are derived from natural sources, such as plants and microorganisms, and work by breaking down pollutants and promoting soil health. Inorganic soil remediation agents, on the other hand, are synthetic substances that chemically react with contaminants to neutralize or remove them from the soil. Both types aim to remove or reduce harmful substances in the soil, but organic agents are considered more environmentally friendly and sustainable.</p></p>

<p><p>Soil remediation agents are utilized in various applications to restore contaminated soil. Loam, clay, silt, sand, and other soil types are commonly treated using these agents. Clay soils are typically compacted, necessitating the use of agents to improve aeration and nutrient availability. Silt soils are prone to erosion and benefit from agents that increase stability and drainage. Sand soils, while well-drained, may lack essential nutrients, thus requiring agents to enhance fertility.
