<p><p>The residential connected lighting market can be categorized into several types, including WiFi, ZigBee, Z-Wave, Bluetooth, and Thread. WiFi enables wireless internet connectivity, allowing users to control their lighting through smartphone apps. ZigBee is a low-power wireless communication technology that supports various controls and standardizes smart lighting devices. Z-Wave also provides wireless communication for home automation, offering a wide range of lighting control options. Bluetooth facilitates direct communication between devices, enabling control of lighting fixtures through smartphones or voice assistants. Thread is a network protocol designed for low-power IoT devices, ensuring reliable and secure connectivity for residential lighting solutions.</p></p>
