<p><p>The food flavoring market is divided into two types: natural and synthetic. Natural flavorings are derived from plants, animals, or microbial sources, and they provide a more authentic taste to food products. They are often labeled as "natural flavors" on ingredient lists. On the other hand, synthetic flavorings are chemically produced in laboratories and are designed to mimic natural flavors. They offer a cost-effective and consistent flavor profile to food products. Both types have their own advantages and are widely used in the food industry to enhance taste and create unique flavors.</p></p>
