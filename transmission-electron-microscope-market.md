<p><p>The transmission electron microscope (TEM) market is divided into three main types. The conventional TEM utilizes a high voltage beam to transmit electrons through a sample, providing 2D images with high resolution. The low-voltage electron microscope operates under lower voltages, suitable for delicate samples such as biological materials. Cryo-microscopy is used to study samples at extremely low temperatures, allowing for the preservation of samples in their native state. These market types cater to different needs in electron microscopy, offering specific capabilities for various applications.</p></p>
