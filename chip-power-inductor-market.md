<p><p>The chip power inductor market consists of two main types: non-shielded chip power inductors and shielded chip power inductors. Non-shielded chip power inductors are smaller in size and provide limited magnetic field shielding. They are generally used in applications where space is a constraint. On the other hand, shielded chip power inductors offer better magnetic field containment and are ideal for applications that require high electromagnetic interference (EMI) suppression. Both types play crucial roles in various electronic devices and circuits, depending on the specific needs and requirements.</p></p>
