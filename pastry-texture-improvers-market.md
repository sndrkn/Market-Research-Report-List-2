<p><p>The market for pastry texture improvers can be categorized into different types. Emulsifiers are substances that help to stabilize the mixture of water and fats, improving the texture of pastry. Enzymes are natural catalysts that enhance dough rising and improve gluten development. Oxidizing agents strengthen the dough structure by increasing oxidation reactions. Reducing agents, on the other hand, reduce the gluten network to create a softer texture in pastry. Lastly, other texture improvers can include additives like stabilizers, dough conditioners, and flavor enhancers.</p></p>
