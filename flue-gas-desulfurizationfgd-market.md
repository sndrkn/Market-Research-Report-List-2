<p><p>Flue Gas Desulfurization (FGD) is a technology used to remove sulfur dioxide (SO2) from flue gases emitted by power plants, industrial facilities, and other sources to comply with environmental regulations. SO2 is a major contributor to acid rain and respiratory issues, making FGD systems crucial for reducing emissions and improving air quality.</p><p>The FGD market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing stringency of emission regulations across the globe. Governments and environmental agencies are imposing stricter limits on sulfur emissions, leading to a rise in the demand for FGD systems. Additionally, the growing awareness about the harmful effects of sulfur emissions on human health and the environment is driving the market.</p><p>The market is also driven by the shift toward cleaner energy sources. As renewable energy and natural gas gain prominence, the demand for FGD systems is expected to increase to mitigate the sulfur emissions from the remaining coal-fired power plants.</p><p>In terms of technology, wet FGD systems dominate the market owing to their high efficiency in removing SO2. However, dry FGD systems are gaining traction due to their lower water consumption and operational flexibility.</p><p>The Asia-Pacific region is expected to witness substantial growth in the FGD market due to the rapid industrialization and growing energy needs in countries like China and India. North America and Europe are also significant markets for FGD systems, driven by stringent emission regulations imposed by their respective governments.</p><p>Key trends in the FGD market include the development of advanced FGD technologies, such as seawater scrubbing and regenerative FGD, to enhance the efficiency of sulfur removal. The integration of FGD systems with other air pollution control technologies, such as selective catalytic reduction (SCR), is another emerging trend.</p><p>In conclusion, the FGD market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 10.1% during the forecast period. Seawater FGD systems use seawater as a reagent. Dry and Semi-dry FGD systems utilize chemicals like lime or sodium carbonate to remove SO2. Lastly, Other FGD systems include ammonia-based and biological FGD systems.</p></p>
