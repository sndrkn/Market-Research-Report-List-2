<p><p>The Artificial Synthetic Dyes market can be broadly categorized into four types: Nitro, Indigo, Aromatic Methane, and Other. </p><p>Nitro dyes are a type of synthetic dye that is produced by a reaction between nitric acid and a compound containing a benzene ring. They are known for their vibrant colors and are widely used in industries such as textiles and printing.</p><p>Indigo dyes are another type of synthetic dye commonly used in the textile industry to produce blue and green hues. They are derived from the Indigofera plant and have been used for centuries.</p><p>Aromatic Methane dyes, also known as azo dyes, are manufactured by combining an amine compound and a diazonium salt. They offer a wide range of colorful shades and are extensively used in fabric, leather, and paper industries.</p><p>Lastly, the Other category encompasses various synthetic dyes such as sulfur dyes, vat dyes, and acid dyes, which are used for specific applications and industries based on their chemical properties and color range.</p></p>
